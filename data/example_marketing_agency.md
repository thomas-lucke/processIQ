We run a digital marketing agency with 30 employees. We serve small and mid-sized businesses, mainly managing social media, paid ads, and content production. Our main workflow is the client campaign process — from the moment a client requests a new campaign to when it goes live and gets reported on.

A new campaign starts when a client submits a brief, either via email or through our project management tool. Reading and logging the brief takes about 20 minutes, costs $18 per campaign in staff time, has a 10% error rate (missing info, unclear goals), and involves 1 account manager.

After that the account manager has a kickoff call with the client to align on goals, budget, audience, and timeline. This takes 45 minutes on average, costs $40, has a 5% error rate, and involves 1 account manager and sometimes 1 strategist.

The strategist then develops the campaign strategy — target audience, messaging, channel selection, KPIs. This takes 3 hours, costs $120, has an 8% error rate (strategy not approved on first pass), and involves 1 strategist.

The strategy goes to the client for approval. Active time is 10 minutes to send and track. But waiting for client feedback takes on average 2 days. We include the waiting time here as 16 hours total. Error rate is 30% — meaning nearly a third of strategies need revisions before approval. Involves 1 account manager.

If revisions are needed, the strategist reworks the strategy. This takes 1.5 hours on average, costs $60, has a 15% error rate (sometimes needs a second revision round), and involves 1 strategist.

Once the strategy is approved, the creative team produces the assets — ad copy, visuals, videos depending on the campaign. This takes 8 hours on average, costs $280, has a 12% error rate (assets rejected internally or by client), and involves 2 creatives.

The assets go through internal quality review before the client sees them. This takes 1 hour, costs $50, has a 20% error rate (things get sent back for fixes), and involves 1 senior creative and 1 account manager.

Then assets are sent to the client for sign-off. Active time is 15 minutes. Waiting time averages 1.5 days — we use 12 hours total. Error rate is 25% (client requests changes). Involves 1 account manager.

If the client requests changes, the creative team makes revisions. This takes 2 hours, costs $80, has a 10% error rate, and involves 1 creative.

Once approved, our ad specialist sets up the campaigns in the relevant platforms — Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, etc. This takes 2 hours, costs $90, has a 5% error rate (setup mistakes caught in QA), and involves 1 ad specialist.

Before going live, we do a final technical QA check — tracking pixels, UTM parameters, budget caps, audience targeting. This takes 45 minutes, costs $40, has an 8% error rate, and involves 1 ad specialist.

The campaign goes live. No active staff time here.

At the end of the reporting period (usually monthly), the account manager pulls performance data and writes a report for the client. This takes 2 hours, costs $90, has a 5% error rate, and involves 1 account manager.

The report is sent to the client and a review call is scheduled. The call takes 30 minutes, costs $28, has a 10% error rate (client dissatisfaction requiring follow-up), and involves 1 account manager.

We have 30 staff: 6 account managers, 4 strategists, 10 creatives, 4 ad specialists, 3 project managers, and 3 in management and admin.
